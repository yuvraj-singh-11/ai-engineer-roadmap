# EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

This Employment Agreement (the **“Agreement”**) is made and entered into on this **23rd day of June 2026** (the **“Effective Date”**),

**BY AND BETWEEN**

**LexiMind AI Technologies Private Limited**, a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, having its registered office at WeWork Galaxy, 3rd Floor, 43 Residency Road, Bengaluru, Karnataka 560025, India (hereinafter referred to as the **“Company”**, which expression shall, unless repugnant to the context, include its successors and permitted assigns);

**AND**

**Mr. Arjun Malhotra**, son of Mr. Rajeev Malhotra, residing at Flat No. B-1204, Cedar Heights, HSR Layout, Sector 2, Bengaluru, Karnataka 560102, India (hereinafter referred to as the **“Employee”**, which expression shall, unless repugnant to the context, include his legal heirs and permitted representatives).

The Company and the Employee are hereinafter collectively referred to as the **“Parties”** and individually as a **“Party.”**

---

## 1. PARTIES

1.1 The Company is engaged in the business of developing and commercializing artificial intelligence-enabled legal technology products, including legal drafting automation, contract intelligence platforms, litigation analytics tools, and compliance workflow software.

1.2 The Employee represents that he possesses the qualifications, professional experience, and technical competencies necessary for the role contemplated herein and is willing to enter into employment on the terms set out in this Agreement.

---

## 2. JOB TITLE

2.1 The Company hereby appoints the Employee as **Senior Software Engineer – AI Platforms**.

2.2 The Employee accepts such appointment and agrees to perform duties associated with the role as may be reasonably assigned from time to time in accordance with this Agreement.

2.3 The Employee’s employment shall commence on **01 July 2026** (the **“Joining Date”**), subject to completion of onboarding formalities, background verification, and submission of required documentation.

2.4 The first **six (6) months** from the Joining Date shall constitute a probation period (the **“Probation Period”**) in accordance with Clause 21.5.

---

## 3. DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

3.1 The Employee shall faithfully, diligently, and to the best of his skill perform all duties reasonably associated with the role of Senior Software Engineer – AI Platforms.

3.2 Without limiting the foregoing, the Employee’s core responsibilities shall include:

  3.2.1 design, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance of backend and AI-integrated software systems;

  3.2.2 architecture and implementation of secure, scalable microservices for legal AI products;

  3.2.3 development and optimization of machine learning serving infrastructure and prompt orchestration pipelines;

  3.2.4 code review, mentorship, and technical guidance to junior engineers;

  3.2.5 ensuring adherence to coding standards, secure development lifecycle practices, and internal engineering policies;

  3.2.6 participation in incident response, root-cause analysis, and system reliability initiatives.

3.3 The Employee shall devote full working time, attention, and abilities to the business of the Company and shall not undertake any other employment, consulting engagement, or business activity without prior written approval of the Company.

3.4 The Employee shall comply with all lawful instructions, policies, and procedures of the Company as amended from time to time.

---

## 4. REPORTING STRUCTURE

4.1 The Employee shall report directly to the **Director of Engineering (AI Products)**, or such other person as may be designated by the Company.

4.2 The Company may modify reporting lines, organizational structure, and team assignments based on business requirements, provided such changes do not materially diminish the Employee’s grade or compensation without due process.

---

## 5. WORK LOCATION

5.1 The Employee’s primary place of work shall be the Company’s Bengaluru office at WeWork Galaxy, 3rd Floor, 43 Residency Road, Bengaluru, Karnataka 560025.

5.2 The Company may require travel within India or internationally for project meetings, client engagements, audits, training, or business continuity needs, subject to reimbursement as per Company policy.

---

## 6. REMOTE WORK POLICY

6.1 The Employee may be permitted to work remotely on a hybrid basis up to three (3) working days per week, subject to managerial approval, project requirements, and policy compliance.

6.2 Remote work is a privilege and not a contractual entitlement; the Company may modify or withdraw remote arrangements with reasonable notice based on operational, security, or regulatory considerations.

6.3 While working remotely, the Employee shall:

  6.3.1 maintain secure internet connectivity and approved endpoint protections;

  6.3.2 access Company systems only through authorized channels and virtual private network controls;

  6.3.3 ensure confidentiality of information in home or co-working environments;

  6.3.4 remain available during core hours as set out in Clause 10.

---

## 7. SALARY

7.1 In consideration of services rendered, the Company shall pay the Employee a gross annual fixed salary of **INR 42,00,000** (Indian Rupees Forty-Two Lakh only), payable in equal monthly installments in arrears, subject to statutory deductions including income tax, provident fund, and professional tax where applicable.

7.2 The monthly gross salary shall be **INR 3,50,000** before deductions.

7.3 A detailed compensation break-up shall be provided in Annexure A and may include basic pay, house rent allowance/special allowance, and other components as structured by the Company.

7.4 Compensation shall be reviewed periodically at the Company’s discretion and shall not be deemed automatically subject to annual increment.

---

## 8. BONUS STRUCTURE

8.1 The Employee shall be eligible for an annual performance-linked bonus up to **15% of annual fixed salary** (target bonus), subject to:

  8.1.1 individual performance against defined goals;

  8.1.2 team and business performance metrics;

  8.1.3 continued employment on the date of bonus payout.

8.2 Bonus is discretionary and non-guaranteed unless expressly communicated in writing.

8.3 Where the Employee separates from employment before payout date, no bonus shall be payable unless mandated by Applicable Law or approved in writing by the Company.

---

## 9. ESOP ELIGIBILITY

9.1 Subject to board and shareholder approvals and applicable ESOP scheme rules, the Employee shall be eligible for a one-time grant of up to **12,000 stock options** under the Company’s Employee Stock Option Plan, vesting over four (4) years with a one-year cliff (25% vesting on completion of 12 months from grant date, thereafter monthly/quarterly vesting as per plan terms).

9.2 ESOPs are governed exclusively by the ESOP Plan document, grant letter, and Applicable Law. In case of conflict, the ESOP Plan shall prevail for option-related matters.

9.3 Unvested options shall lapse on cessation of employment except as otherwise provided under the ESOP Plan.

---

## 10. WORKING HOURS

10.1 The Employee’s normal working hours shall be 9.5 hours per workday (inclusive of breaks), Monday through Friday, aligned to business needs.

10.2 Core collaboration hours are 10:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. IST.

10.3 Given the managerial and technical nature of the role, the Employee may be required to work beyond standard hours to meet production deadlines, release windows, incident response obligations, or urgent business requirements, in compliance with Applicable Law.

---

## 11. LEAVE POLICY

11.1 The Employee shall be entitled to paid leave in accordance with Company policy and Applicable Law, currently comprising:

  11.1.1 Privileged/Earned Leave: 18 working days per calendar year (pro-rated for partial year);

  11.1.2 Casual Leave: 6 working days per calendar year;

  11.1.3 Sick Leave: 12 working days per calendar year;

  11.1.4 Public holidays as notified by the Company.

11.2 Leave must be pre-approved except in emergencies or illness, where notice shall be given at the earliest practical opportunity.

11.3 Carry-forward, encashment, and lapse of leave shall be governed by Company policy and statutory requirements.

---

## 12. CONFIDENTIALITY

12.1 During and after employment, the Employee shall maintain strict confidentiality regarding all non-public information relating to the Company, its affiliates, clients, partners, personnel, technology, and operations.

12.2 Confidential information includes source code, model parameters, prompts, legal datasets, pricing strategies, customer contracts, security architecture, and product roadmaps.

12.3 The Employee shall not, without prior written consent, disclose confidential information to any third party or use such information for any purpose other than discharge of official duties.

12.4 This Clause shall survive termination in accordance with Clause 26.

---

## 13. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT

13.1 The Employee acknowledges that all works, inventions, designs, discoveries, developments, improvements, documentation, scripts, models, and other outputs conceived, authored, or reduced to practice by the Employee during employment and relating to the Company’s business (collectively, **“Work Product”**) shall be the sole property of the Company.

13.2 The Employee hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company all rights, title, and interest in and to such Work Product, including copyrights, patents, design rights, database rights, trade secrets, and all analogous rights worldwide.

13.3 Illustrative examples include:

  13.3.1 a contract clause classification model built during a Company sprint;

  13.3.2 a retrieval-augmented generation orchestration engine developed using Company datasets;

  13.3.3 a deployment automation script for legal document redlining services.

13.4 The Employee shall execute all documents and provide assistance reasonably required to perfect, register, enforce, or defend the Company’s IP rights, even after cessation of employment, with reasonable out-of-pocket expenses reimbursed.

---

## 14. NON-COMPETE

14.1 During employment, the Employee shall not directly or indirectly engage in any business competing with the Company’s core business of AI-driven legal technology platforms, contract intelligence products, legal workflow automation, and allied services.

14.2 Post-termination, the Employee shall comply with restrictions enforceable under Applicable Law. The Parties acknowledge that post-employment restraints shall be interpreted to the extent permissible under Indian law.

14.3 Nothing in this Clause prevents passive holding of publicly listed securities not exceeding 2% of voting rights in any competing entity.

---

## 15. NON-SOLICITATION

15.1 During employment and for twelve (12) months following termination, the Employee shall not directly or indirectly solicit or induce:

  15.1.1 any employee, consultant, or contractor of the Company with whom the Employee had material interaction in the preceding twelve months, to terminate or reduce engagement with the Company;

  15.1.2 any customer or prospective customer of the Company to cease, reduce, or divert business from the Company;

  15.1.3 any vendor or strategic partner to materially alter existing commercial terms to the detriment of the Company.

15.2 General solicitations through broad public advertisements not specifically targeting Company personnel shall not constitute breach of this Clause.

---

## 16. DATA PROTECTION

16.1 The Employee shall process all personal data and sensitive business data in accordance with Applicable Law, Company policies, and documented instructions.

16.2 The Employee shall promptly report suspected personal data breaches, unauthorized access, or policy deviations to the designated security and legal contacts.

16.3 The Employee shall use only approved tools and repositories for storage and processing of Company or customer data and shall not transfer data to personal devices or unauthorized cloud services.

---

## 17. CODE OWNERSHIP

17.1 All code, scripts, libraries, modules, APIs, build pipelines, test suites, and technical documentation created, modified, or contributed by the Employee within the scope of employment, on Company systems, or using Company resources shall vest exclusively in the Company.

17.2 The Employee shall not assert lien, ownership, moral-right objections (to the extent waivable), or usage restrictions over such code.

17.3 Personal pre-existing code brought by the Employee into Company systems must be pre-declared in writing and approved; otherwise it shall be presumed to be Work Product under Clause 13.

---

## 18. OPEN SOURCE USAGE POLICY

18.1 The Employee shall comply with the Company’s Open Source Software (OSS) Policy, including approval workflows for introducing third-party components.

18.2 The Employee shall not incorporate copyleft or restrictive-license code (including GPL-family licenses or similar reciprocal licenses) into proprietary repositories without express written legal and security clearance.

18.3 The Employee shall maintain accurate software bill-of-materials records where required and ensure proper attribution and license notices for approved OSS usage.

18.4 Any external code contribution made in the Employee’s capacity with the Company requires prior written authorization.

---

## 19. PERFORMANCE REVIEWS

19.1 The Employee’s performance shall be reviewed periodically, typically semi-annually, against role expectations, engineering quality metrics, delivery outcomes, collaboration standards, and leadership contributions.

19.2 The Company may establish a Performance Improvement Plan (**“PIP”**) where performance materially falls below expectations.

19.3 Failure to satisfactorily complete a PIP within prescribed timelines may lead to disciplinary measures, including reassignment, withholding discretionary benefits, or termination under Clause 21.

---

## 20. DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS

20.1 The Company may take disciplinary action for misconduct, poor performance, policy violation, or breach of this Agreement.

20.2 Misconduct includes, without limitation:

  20.2.1 theft, fraud, embezzlement, or falsification of records;

  20.2.2 unauthorized disclosure of confidential information or source code;

  20.2.3 harassment, discrimination, or workplace violence;

  20.2.4 deliberate security violations, credential sharing, or unauthorized system access;

  20.2.5 intoxication at work or possession of prohibited substances at workplace;

  20.2.6 conflict of interest, bribery, or acceptance of unlawful gratifications.

20.3 Disciplinary actions may include warning letters, suspension, loss of privileges, demotion where lawful, or termination depending on severity and recurrence.

---

## 21. TERMINATION BY EMPLOYER

21.1 The Company may terminate employment without cause by providing **ninety (90) days’ notice** in writing or salary in lieu of notice, in whole or part, subject to Applicable Law and Clause 23.

21.2 The Company may terminate employment with immediate effect, without notice or payment in lieu (to the extent legally permissible), for cause including gross misconduct, fraud, willful disobedience, material breach of confidentiality/IP obligations, or criminal conviction involving moral turpitude.

21.3 The Company may terminate employment on grounds of redundancy, role elimination, restructuring, long-term incapacity, or business exigency, with notice and statutory dues as applicable.

21.4 During notice period, the Company may require the Employee to:

  21.4.1 continue active duties;

  21.4.2 serve a garden-leave period with restricted system access;

  21.4.3 complete transition, documentation, and handover obligations.

21.5 During the **Probation Period** of six (6) months, either Party may terminate employment by giving thirty (30) days’ notice or salary in lieu, unless termination is for cause.

---

## 22. TERMINATION BY EMPLOYEE

22.1 After confirmation of employment, the Employee may resign by giving the Company **ninety (90) days’ prior written notice**.

22.2 During probation, resignation notice shall be thirty (30) days unless waived by the Company.

22.3 The Company may, at its discretion, accept shorter notice subject to recovery of shortfall in notice period salary and completion of satisfactory handover.

22.4 The Employee shall return all Company property, credentials, documents, and assets on or before last working day and comply with exit procedures.

---

## 23. SEVERANCE

23.1 In case of termination without cause under Clause 21.1, the Employee shall be entitled to:

  23.1.1 salary and benefits up to last working day;

  23.1.2 payment in lieu of unserved notice period, where applicable;

  23.1.3 accrued but unused earned leave encashment as per policy and law;

  23.1.4 statutory severance/retrenchment compensation, if and to the extent required by Applicable Law.

23.2 No severance shall be payable in cases of termination for cause under Clause 21.2, except statutory dues mandatorily payable under law.

23.3 Any severance or ex gratia payment may be made conditional upon execution of a full and final settlement and release agreement.

---

## 24. GOVERNING LAW (INDIA)

24.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India.

24.2 Subject to Clause 25, courts at Bengaluru, Karnataka shall have jurisdiction for interim and supervisory matters.

---

## 25. ARBITRATION

25.1 Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, including questions of interpretation, validity, breach, or termination, shall first be attempted to be resolved amicably within thirty (30) days.

25.2 Failing amicable resolution, the dispute shall be referred to arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 (as amended).

25.3 The tribunal shall consist of a sole arbitrator appointed mutually by the Parties; failing consensus, appointment shall be made in accordance with law.

25.4 The seat and venue of arbitration shall be **Bengaluru, India**.

25.5 The language of arbitration shall be English. The award shall be final and binding.

25.6 Nothing herein prevents the Company from seeking urgent injunctive or equitable relief from competent courts for protection of confidentiality, data, or intellectual property.

---

## 26. SURVIVAL CLAUSES

26.1 The following clauses shall survive termination or expiry of employment: Clause 12 (Confidentiality), Clause 13 (Intellectual Property Assignment), Clause 15 (Non-Solicitation), Clause 16 (Data Protection), Clause 17 (Code Ownership), Clause 18 (Open Source Usage obligations to the extent applicable), Clause 24 (Governing Law), Clause 25 (Arbitration), and this Clause 26.

26.2 Termination of employment shall not affect rights accrued prior to termination or obligations which by nature are intended to continue.

---

## 27. GENERAL PROVISIONS

27.1 This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties concerning employment terms and supersedes prior discussions and correspondence.

27.2 Any amendment shall be valid only if made in writing and signed by both Parties.

27.3 If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, remaining provisions shall continue in full force, and the invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent required to make it enforceable.

27.4 Failure to enforce any provision shall not constitute waiver of future enforcement rights.

27.5 The Employee confirms that entering into this Agreement does not breach any prior contractual obligations.

---
