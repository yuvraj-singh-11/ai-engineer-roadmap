NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT (NDA)

This NDA is entered into on 10 February 2025 between LexLegis Private Limited ("Disclosing Party") and Delta Review Associates ("Receiving Party").

1. Confidential Information
"Confidential Information" includes contracts, legal strategies, client data, pricing, internal policies, and any non-public business information disclosed in oral, written, or electronic form.

2. Confidentiality Obligations
The Receiving Party shall:
- Keep Confidential Information strictly confidential.
- Use Confidential Information solely for permitted business purposes.
- Restrict access to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis.
- Apply reasonable security controls to prevent unauthorized disclosure.
- Promptly notify the Disclosing Party of any suspected breach.

3. Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that:
- Is publicly available without breach;
- Was lawfully known before disclosure;
- Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information;
- Is disclosed under legal compulsion, with prior notice where permitted.

4. Term and Survival
This NDA remains effective for 3 years from the effective date.
Confidentiality obligations survive termination for 5 years.

5. Return/Destruction
Upon request or termination, the Receiving Party shall return or securely destroy all Confidential Information and confirm completion in writing.

6. Liability
Unauthorized disclosure may cause irreparable harm. The Disclosing Party may seek injunctive relief and damages as available by law.

7. Governing Law
This NDA is governed by Indian law, with courts in New Delhi having jurisdiction.
