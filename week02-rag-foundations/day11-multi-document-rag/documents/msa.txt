# MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Master Services Agreement (the **“Agreement”**) is made and entered into on **23 June 2026** (the **“Effective Date”**)

**BY AND BETWEEN**

**PrismForge Technologies Private Limited**, a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, with registered office at 12th Floor, One BKC Tower, Bandra Kurla Complex, Mumbai, Maharashtra 400051, India (**“Vendor”**);

and

**Nexora Enterprises Limited**, a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, with corporate office at 8th Floor, Meridian Business Park, Outer Ring Road, Bengaluru, Karnataka 560103, India (**“Customer”**).

Vendor and Customer are each a **“Party”** and together the **“Parties.”**

## 1. Parties
1.1 This Agreement governs Customer’s purchase and Vendor’s provision of AI-enabled software services and related professional services as described herein and in each Statement of Work.  
1.2 Affiliates may procure Services under this Agreement by executing separate SOWs referencing this Agreement.

## 2. Definitions
2.1 **“Services”** means the SaaS platform, API access, support, and professional services provided by Vendor.  
2.2 **“Platform”** means Vendor’s hosted AI contract intelligence and workflow automation product branded “PrismForge LegalAI Cloud.”  
2.3 **“SOW”** means a Statement of Work signed by both Parties.  
2.4 **“Deliverables”** means tangible work product identified in an SOW.  
2.5 **“Service Levels”** means uptime and support commitments in Section 5.  
2.6 **“Customer Data”** means all data submitted by or on behalf of Customer to the Services.  
2.7 **“ACV”** means annual contract value payable by Customer for subscription Services.  
2.8 **“Business Day”** means a day other than Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday in Mumbai, India.

## 3. Scope of Services
3.1 Vendor shall provide:  
(a) subscription-based access to the Platform on a SaaS basis;  
(b) API access for integration with Customer systems, subject to rate limits and technical documentation;  
(c) implementation, onboarding, configuration, and training services as set out in applicable SOWs;  
(d) maintenance, support, and updates.  
3.2 Customer receives a non-exclusive, non-transferable, non-sublicensable right during the Term to use the Services for internal business purposes.

## 4. Statements of Work
4.1 Specific services, timelines, milestones, assumptions, dependencies, and fees shall be defined in one or more SOWs.  
4.2 In case of conflict, order of precedence shall be: (i) signed SOW; (ii) this Agreement; (iii) exhibits/policies referenced herein.  
4.3 No SOW is binding unless executed by authorized signatories of both Parties.

## 5. Service Levels
5.1 Vendor commits to **99.9% monthly uptime** for production SaaS Services, excluding Scheduled Maintenance (not exceeding 6 hours/month) and Excluded Downtime.  
5.2 If monthly uptime falls below 99.9%, Customer shall be entitled to service credits as follows:  
(a) 99.0% to <99.9%: 5% of monthly subscription fee;  
(b) 98.0% to <99.0%: 10%;  
(c) <98.0%: 15%.  
5.3 Total credits in any month are capped at 15% of monthly subscription fees and are Customer’s sole remedy for SLA breach, without prejudice to termination rights under Section 26 for chronic failure.  
5.4 Severity 1 incidents (production outage/security-critical) shall have 30-minute response and continuous remediation efforts.

## 6. Deliverables
6.1 Vendor shall deliver Deliverables in accordance with the applicable SOW and industry standards.  
6.2 Unless expressly stated, Deliverables are deemed accepted upon completion of Acceptance Criteria under Section 12.

## 7. Customer Responsibilities
7.1 Customer shall:  
(a) provide timely access to personnel, systems, and information reasonably required;  
(b) ensure legality, accuracy, and rights to use Customer Data;  
(c) maintain secure credentials and notify Vendor of unauthorized use;  
(d) appoint a project manager for governance and approvals.  
7.2 Delays caused by Customer dependencies may result in equitable timeline adjustments.

## 8. Vendor Responsibilities
8.1 Vendor shall perform Services in a professional and workmanlike manner using qualified personnel.  
8.2 Vendor shall maintain helpdesk support, monitoring, incident management, and change management practices.  
8.3 Vendor shall not materially degrade core functionality during the Subscription Term.

## 9. Fees and Payment Terms
9.1 Customer shall pay fees set out in each SOW/order form.  
9.2 Initial commercial terms: ACV of **INR 2,40,00,000** for 12-month subscription, billed quarterly in advance; one-time implementation fee of **INR 30,00,000**.  
9.3 Payment is due within **30 days** from undisputed invoice date.  
9.4 Late undisputed payments may incur interest at 1.5% per month or maximum lawful rate, whichever is lower.  
9.5 Fees are non-cancelable and non-refundable except as expressly provided.

## 10. Taxes
10.1 Fees exclude GST and other applicable indirect taxes, which shall be charged additionally.  
10.2 Each Party is responsible for taxes imposed on its income, payroll, and corporate existence.  
10.3 If withholding tax applies, Customer may deduct and remit as legally required and shall provide valid withholding certificates.

## 11. Change Requests
11.1 Either Party may propose changes to scope, timelines, deliverables, or assumptions via written Change Request.  
11.2 Vendor shall provide impact assessment on fees, schedule, and resources.  
11.3 Changes become effective only upon signed Change Order.

## 12. Acceptance Criteria
12.1 Each SOW shall specify objective Acceptance Criteria and testing procedure.  
12.2 Customer shall conduct acceptance testing within 10 Business Days of delivery.  
12.3 If Deliverables materially fail Acceptance Criteria, Customer shall provide written rejection reasons; Vendor shall remediate and resubmit.  
12.4 Deliverables shall be deemed accepted if Customer uses them in production or fails to respond within acceptance window.

## 13. Warranties
13.1 Vendor warrants that during the Term:  
(a) Services will materially conform to documentation;  
(b) Services will be performed with reasonable skill and care;  
(c) Vendor has authority to grant rights under this Agreement.  
13.2 Customer warrants it has rights to provide Customer Data and that such use does not violate Applicable Law.  
13.3 Except as expressly stated, Services are provided “as is,” and all implied warranties are disclaimed to the maximum extent permitted by law.

## 14. Intellectual Property
14.1 Vendor retains all rights in the Platform, software, models, APIs, documentation, and pre-existing materials.  
14.2 Customer retains all rights in Customer Data and Customer trademarks.  
14.3 Subject to this Agreement, Vendor may use aggregated and de-identified telemetry for service improvement, provided no Customer-identifiable data is disclosed.  
14.4 Custom Deliverables specifically paid for under an SOW shall be licensed to Customer on a perpetual, non-exclusive basis unless otherwise stated.

## 15. Confidentiality
15.1 Each Party shall protect the other Party’s Confidential Information using at least reasonable care and not less than the care used for its own similar information.  
15.2 Confidential Information shall be used solely to perform or receive Services.  
15.3 Confidentiality obligations shall not apply to information that is public, independently developed, rightfully received from third party, or required by law to be disclosed (with prior notice where lawful).  
15.4 Obligations survive for 5 years from disclosure.

## 16. Data Processing
16.1 Vendor shall process Customer Data solely on documented instructions from Customer and only for providing Services.  
16.2 Vendor shall store primary production Customer Data in data centers located in **India** and shall not transfer identifiable Customer Data outside India without prior written consent.  
16.3 Parties shall execute a Data Processing Addendum where required by Applicable Law.

## 17. Security Requirements
17.1 Vendor shall maintain an information security program aligned to ISO 27001 or equivalent controls, including:  
(a) encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256 or equivalent);  
(b) role-based access control and MFA for privileged users;  
(c) vulnerability management and annual penetration testing;  
(d) logging, monitoring, and secure backup.  
17.2 Vendor shall maintain least-privilege access and periodic access recertification.

## 18. Cybersecurity Incident Response
18.1 Vendor shall notify Customer of a confirmed cybersecurity incident affecting Customer Data without undue delay, and in any event within **24 hours** of confirmation.  
18.2 Vendor shall provide incident details, containment actions, remediation plan, and periodic updates.  
18.3 Vendor shall cooperate with Customer’s reasonable forensic, regulatory, and contractual response obligations.

## 19. Compliance Requirements
19.1 Each Party shall comply with Applicable Law, including anti-bribery, sanctions, export controls, labor, tax, and data protection laws.  
19.2 Vendor shall maintain policies and training reasonably designed to ensure ongoing compliance.  
19.3 Customer shall not use Services for unlawful, infringing, or prohibited content generation activities.

## 20. Audit Rights
20.1 Customer may, no more than once annually, audit Vendor’s compliance with Sections 16–19 upon 30 days’ notice, during business hours, and subject to confidentiality safeguards.  
20.2 In lieu of on-site audit, Vendor may provide recent independent audit reports/certifications (e.g., ISO/SOC) where sufficient.  
20.3 Customer bears audit costs unless material non-compliance is identified, in which case Vendor shall bear reasonable remediation verification costs.

## 21. Indemnification
21.1 Vendor shall defend and indemnify Customer against third-party claims alleging Services infringe intellectual property rights, and pay resulting damages/costs finally awarded or settled with Vendor consent.  
21.2 Vendor may, at its option, procure rights, modify Services, or replace infringing components; if not commercially reasonable, Vendor may terminate affected Services and refund prepaid unused fees for terminated portion.  
21.3 Customer shall defend and indemnify Vendor against third-party claims arising from Customer Data or Customer’s unlawful use of Services.  
21.4 Indemnified Party shall promptly notify, provide cooperation, and allow control of defense to indemnifying Party.

## 22. Limitation of Liability
22.1 Except for Excluded Claims, each Party’s aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed **100% of fees paid or payable in the 12 months preceding the event giving rise to liability**.  
22.2 Neither Party is liable for indirect, consequential, special, exemplary, or punitive damages, including lost profits or goodwill.  
22.3 **Excluded Claims**: breach of confidentiality, data protection obligations, indemnification liabilities, fraud, willful misconduct, and unpaid fees.

## 23. Insurance
23.1 Vendor shall maintain during the Term:  
(a) Professional Indemnity / Errors & Omissions: INR 10,00,00,000 per claim;  
(b) Cyber Liability: INR 15,00,00,000 per claim;  
(c) Commercial General Liability: INR 5,00,00,000 per claim;  
(d) Workers’ compensation as required by law.  
23.2 Certificates of insurance shall be furnished upon request.

## 24. Force Majeure
24.1 Neither Party is liable for delay/failure caused by events beyond reasonable control, excluding payment obligations.  
24.2 Affected Party shall notify promptly and mitigate impacts.  
24.3 If force majeure continues beyond 60 days, either Party may terminate affected Services on written notice.

## 25. Suspension of Services
25.1 Vendor may suspend Services, upon prior notice where practicable, for:  
(a) material security risk;  
(b) Customer’s unlawful use;  
(c) non-payment exceeding 30 days after written reminder;  
(d) emergency maintenance.  
25.2 Vendor shall restore Services promptly once grounds for suspension are cured.

## 26. Termination for Cause
26.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or an SOW for material breach not cured within 30 days after written notice.  
26.2 Customer may terminate for cause if Vendor fails Service Levels for 3 consecutive months or 4 months in any rolling 12-month period.  
26.3 Upon termination for Vendor breach, Customer is entitled to refund of prepaid unused subscription fees for terminated period.

## 27. Termination for Convenience
27.1 Customer may terminate an SOW for convenience after initial 12-month committed period, with 90 days’ written notice.  
27.2 Vendor may terminate for convenience only if all affected Services are sunset across similarly situated customers, with at least 180 days’ notice and transition support under Section 28.

## 28. Transition Assistance
28.1 For up to 6 months post-termination, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance at then-current professional services rates.  
28.2 Vendor shall enable export of Customer Data in commonly used machine-readable format and certify deletion of residual production data, subject to legal retention obligations.

## 29. Governing Law (India)
29.1 This Agreement is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India.

## 30. Arbitration (Mumbai)
30.1 Disputes not resolved amicably within 30 days shall be finally resolved by arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 (as amended).  
30.2 Seat and venue: **Mumbai, Maharashtra, India**.  
30.3 Tribunal: sole arbitrator jointly appointed; failing agreement, appointed per law.  
30.4 Language: English.  
30.5 Nothing prevents either Party from seeking interim injunctive relief from courts in Mumbai.

## 31. Survival
31.1 Sections 9 (accrued payment obligations), 14, 15, 16, 18, 21, 22, 28, 29, 30, 31, and 32 survive expiry or termination.
